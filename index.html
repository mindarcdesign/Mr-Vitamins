📊 Review Methodology

This assessment reviews key pages across desktop and mobile breakpoints, identifying design strengths and weaknesses for each layout. Pages evaluated: Home, Product Listing, Product Detail, Cart, Blog Listing, and Blog.

This review was conducted from a visual aesthetic and enhancement lens. Much of the recommendations have a subjective quality to them. The review also takes into account the brand guidelines; as a matter of fact, there are no comments recommending changes to brand colors or brand fonts. The review is also under the assumption that this is primarily an e-commerce site focused on conversion and sales, and less focused on brand voice and visual experience. So we have opted not to recommend things that would involve a high level of creative content creation and management. It was also with the understanding that this is a marketplace site and control over the images is limited, unlike a brand site.

🟡 Friction

  • Category Pill Spacing The vertical gap between the main header/image area and the category navigation "pills" is inconsistent with the spacing below them. The current spacing feels tight and unbalanced.

  • FAQ Text Weight & Scale The typography for the FAQ accordion headers is overly large and heavy (bold weight). This creates excessive visual dominance, making the section feel dense and "shouty."
    Make the FAQ headings Medium not bold 
  • FAQ Section Layout The FAQ section is constrained to the same width as the product index column rather than the full page width. It lacks visual distinction (neutral background) and adequate spacing, blending into the product list.
    Make this section full-width and more visually separated from the supplement information via differnt colour and frameing
  • SEO Text Presentation (Wall of Text) The educational content (e.g., "Types of Vitamin C Supplements") is presented as a long, unstyled bulleted list. This "wall of text" forces users to scroll excessively and is visually unengaging.
    Make this section full-width and more visually separated from the supplement information via differnt colour and frameing

🟢 Opportunity

  • Heading Line Spacing The primary page heading on the Product Listing Page (PLP) has tight line spacing (leading), making the text feel dense and less "editorial."
    Increase the line height for headings to 150% (don't apply this to single line headings )
  • Body Copy Line Spacing The paragraph text in the PLP header description (e.g., "Discover our comprehensive range...") has tight leading. Increasing this to 150% would significantly improve readability and reduce visual density.
    Increase the line height by 10%
  • Header Separator Styling The solid black border line below the header content sits awkwardly against the rounded bottom corners of the hero image, creating a visual disconnection ("notch") rather than a seamless transition.
    Either remove the black divider line or remove the radii on the bottom left and right of the image block.
  • Sort Dropdown Width The "Featured" sort dropdown lacks a defined maximum width, causing it to appear unnecessarily wide relative to its content. This creates a loose, unpolished visual appearance.
    set the max width to 200px
  • Product Card Styling & Utility The product cards use a hard keyline stroke (border) adding visual noise. They also lack a direct "Add to Cart" action and distinct background for the product image, making the interface feel less transactional.
    A/B test a design option with an always-on "Add to Cart" and a neutral-coloured card that allows you to remove the stroke. This would require the images to be deep-etched if they are not already.

Cart

🔴 Critical

🟡 Friction

  • Competing Primary Buttons ("Continue Shopping") The "Continue Shopping" button currently uses a high-contrast, primary button style (red background). This visually matches the "Checkout" button, creating two competing primary actions. This violates the principle of visual hierarchy. The primary goal of the Cart page is conversion (Checkout).
    "Continue Shopping" is a secondary action and should be visually demoted (e.g., using an outlined "ghost" button style or a simple text link) to ensure the path to purchase is the singular, dominant focus.
  • Inefficient Fulfillment Block Placement The "Delivery / Click & Collect" selection block is currently positioned at the top of the main content column. This pushes the actual cart items (product list) further down the page, potentially below the fold on smaller screens.
    It is recommended to create an option that moves the Delivery / Click & Collect block into the right-hand sidebar, positioned just above or within the Order Summary. This layout shift will allow the product list to move up, ensuring the user's cart contents are immediately visible and at the top upon landing on the page.
  • Product Price Vertical Misalignment The price element within the cart product row appears vertically misaligned (not centered) when compared to the corresponding product title and the quantity (Qty) selector. This creates an unbalanced and unpolished appearance for the product list.
    It is recommended to ensure the price element is vertically centered (middle-aligned) with the product title and quantity input field to improve visual symmetry and scannability across the product row.

🟢 Opportunity

  • Free Shipping Progress Bar Placement The free shipping progress bar and messaging are currently placed outside of the main Order Summary panel.
    It is recommended to create a alterative design that moves this element and integrate it directly into the Order Summary block. Grouping this information within the conversion-focused area aligns the primary cost incentive (free shipping) with the user's final decision point.
  • Inconsistent Border Radius (Fulfillment Block) The border radius (corner rounding) on the top of the fulfillment selection block (Delivery / Click & Collect) is either missing or inconsistent with other rounded elements on the page.
    It is recommended to use the standard border radius used on the other corners of this block to match the site's established design system. This minor fix will improve the visual polish of the cart interface.
  • Unnecessary Wishlist Action in Cart The "Add to Wishlist" (heart icon) is currently displayed on product lines within the cart. Once an item is in the cart, the user's intent is high. Offering a "Wishlist" option at this stage creates a distraction and effectively encourages users to not buy the item now (save it for later).
    It is recommended to remove this action from the cart view to reduce clutter and focus entirely on conversion/checkout

Header

🔴 Critical

🟡 Friction

  • Lack of Sticky Navigation The header currently remains static at the top of the page. As the user scrolls down to browse products, they lose access to primary navigation, search, and the cart.
    It is recommended to implement a Sticky Header that shrinks in size after scroll or a scroll-up-to-reveal header that keeps the navigation bar fixed to the top of the viewport. This ensures key actions are always accessible without requiring the user to scroll back to the top.

    It is recommended to consolidate this into a single, consistent location (preferably the heart icon in the top utility bar, following standard e-commerce patterns) to clean up the layout.
